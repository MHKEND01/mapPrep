One mistake people can make when creating data visualizations as maps is using a misleading projection. As the Earth is spherical, no two-dimensional projection can accurately map the planet. There will inevitably be distortion in either shape, area, angles, distance, and direction. You don’t want to use a certain map projection if it may distort the message of your visualization. At the very least, readers should be aware of these distortions. For instance, in grade school I unthinkingly assumed that Greenland was a massive continent because I had seen projections like these in school textbooks. Mercator projections preserve the shapes of continents, but grossly distort relative sizes. In reality, Greenland is about one-third the size of Australia. I failed to realize that for a long time because textbook writers and teachers failed to highlight the distortions of the Mercator projection.
